Overview of AMR

Confronting a Global Threat

When bacteria and fungi develop the ability to resist treatments that initially killed them, this is known as antimicrobial resistance, or AMR. This change increases the likelihood of serious consequences and increases disease duration by making common infections more difficult to treat.

AMR, also referred to as a hidden pandemic, poses a threat to the foundation for modern medicine. The hazards of routine operations, chemotherapy, and organ transplantation increases significantly in the absence of sufficient antimicrobials.

The goal of this platform is to encourage cooperation and raise awareness. In order to tackle the urgent problem of AMR, we want to gather together scientists, medical professionals, and ordinary people.
