Rational Antibiotic Use

Only use antibiotics as prescribed by a licensed health care provider. Avoid self-care/medications unless directed to do so by a health care professional. Self-care, other than what a health care provider has prescribed, can lead to unnecessary use of antibiotics and consequently lead to resistance to the antibiotics.

Infection Prevention

Infection prevention includes good hygiene practices, immunization of individuals at risk, and sanitation measures. The less infections that occur, the less antibiotics will be used and, as a consequence, less dissemination of antibiotic resistant bacteria.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

Implement healthcare delivery programs to promote prudent decisions for antibiotic prescribing. This will help make sure the appropriate antibiotic with the appropriate dosing levels for the appropriate duration can help lower the amount of misuse and overuse.

Surveillance & Monitoring

conduct a surveillance program that monitors the utilization of antimicrobials and antimicrobial resistance to inform antibiotic prescribing policy/decisions, direct treatment protocols for patients, inform public health preparedness for new and emerging threat/organism trends, etc.

Public & Healthcare Education

Cultivate an awareness with patients, publicly, and health care workers to apply healthy change regarding multiplicity of layers that impact antimicrobial resistance and the harmful consequences as a result of misuse or overuse of antibiotics.

Improved Diagnostics

Use rapid diagnostic tests to identify infections by specific infectious organism, including resistance patterns, to direct therapy with a specific or narrow spectrum of therapy and avoid broad-spectrum use of antibiotics.
